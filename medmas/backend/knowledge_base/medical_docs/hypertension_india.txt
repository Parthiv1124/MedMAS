Hypertension (High Blood Pressure) in India
India has approximately 214 million people with hypertension.
Hypertension is defined as systolic BP ≥130 mmHg or diastolic BP ≥80 mmHg (AHA 2017).
ICMR definition still uses ≥140/90 mmHg for Indian clinical practice.
Stages: Normal <120/80, Elevated 120-129/<80, Stage 1: 130-139/80-89, Stage 2: ≥140/≥90.
Symptoms: Usually silent. May include headache, blurred vision, chest pain, dizziness.
Hypertensive crisis: Systolic >180 or diastolic >120 — requires immediate medical attention.
Risk factors: Age, obesity, high salt intake, sedentary lifestyle, smoking, diabetes, family history.
Complications: Stroke, heart attack, heart failure, kidney disease, vision loss.
Treatment: Lifestyle changes + antihypertensive medications. ACE inhibitors, ARBs, CCBs, diuretics.
Monitoring: Check BP at least every 6 months if normal; monthly if on treatment.
