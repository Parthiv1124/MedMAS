Mental Health in India: Key Facts
India has 197.3 million people with mental disorders (National Mental Health Survey 2016).
Depression affects 45.7 million, anxiety disorders affect 44.9 million people in India.
Treatment gap: 83% of people with severe mental disorders receive no treatment.
Suicide: Leading cause of death in age group 15-29 in India (WHO 2023).
Suicide rate: 12 per 100,000 population in India.
Common conditions: Depression, anxiety, bipolar disorder, schizophrenia, substance use.
Stigma is the primary barrier to seeking treatment in India.
Rural-urban disparity: Rural areas have <1 psychiatrist per million population.
Crisis resources: iCall (9152987821), Vandrevala Foundation (1860-2662-345), SNEHI (044-24640050).
CBT (Cognitive Behavioural Therapy) is evidence-based and effective for depression and anxiety.
WHO mhGAP guidelines recommend task-sharing mental health care with non-specialist health workers.
ASHA workers trained in mental health first aid under NMHP (National Mental Health Programme).
Warning signs of crisis: Expressing hopelessness, withdrawal from activities, giving away possessions, talking about death.
